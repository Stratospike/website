The mark of any good DevOps implementation is how widely it is adopted and there are three core tenants that all good implementations share:

Reproducibility

The root cause of many business problems is the lack of reproducibility:
  • Unpredictable deployment failures
  • Lack of agility / unwillingness to release often
  • Loss of knowledge due to staff turnover

Automation is the ideal solution to all of these problems, however automating everything may not be realistic.

Visibility

Keeping things simple, is usually the best option for smaller companies. However as companies grow, so do the number of components in their infrastructure. With the extra components comes a need for more visibility into many areas, including: showing the current state of the infrastructure, recent changes including auditing records providing details on when, why and who made the change.

Historical metrics, including system performance, key business metrics and process metrics, such as which modules cause the most test failures.

Flexibility

Many organisations struggle to maintain velocity as they try to introduce reproducibility, for two reasons:

  • Not enough visibility into either the development process and/or the deployment pipeline
  • Reproducibility is achieved through rigid processes and/or tooling

In order to avoid these problems it critical to analyze where the choke points are and deliver solutions that address the root cause of the problem, without introducing additional friction.
